Spring Boot (3) to Boost Productivity

  • Every once in a while, when we drive, as drivers of our cars, we do little work compared to what the car itself does to move us.
  • We press the Gas Pedal and the Car does a lot, we press the break pedal and again the Car does a lot.
  • However, unlie driving, enterprise application development even using Spring Framework was still lots of work
  • Applications across business verticals from Banking, to Retail to Healthcare need Database Connectivity, Message Broker Connectivity for example
  • In Spring Framework, we worked a lot more just to configure these Database connections, message broker connecitons
  • That effort took away from our primary focus which is writing business logic
  • In 2012, Spring (Pivotal) realized that how much redundant work can be saved from enginners and released Spring Boot
  • While traditional Spring MVC gave us war files that we needed to deploy to our self downloaded Tomcat / Jetty server, Spring Boot came with its own default Tomcat Servlet Containers
  • Plus it introduced convention over configuration and if certain jars (database drivers and jpa starters) were present on the classpath, Spring Boot autoconfigured those using simple properties
  • The time we needed to set the application up before we started writing business logic, got reduced significantly!
  • Giving us instant boost in Productivity
  • However, Spring removed the "s" from Boost and called it Boot to simulate the bootstrapping process made simpler
  • In Aviation, for example Airlines provides the service to transport passengers and goods from one place to another
  • But all Airlines need security services that the Government (TSA in the US) provides, freeing Airlines to focus on travel excellence which is core business
  • Likewise Spring Boot frees Banks and Retail Organizations (and others) to write their business logic while Spring Boot provides general services security, DB connectivity etc
